Macro & Market-Wide Breaking News
These feeds capture "regime shifts"—things that move the entire S&P 500 or global bond markets instantly.
* Investing.com (Breaking News): This is the most active "real-time" feed available for free. It covers sudden spikes in oil, war updates, and Fed leaks.
    * URL: https://www.investing.com/rss/news.rss (General)
    * URL: https://www.investing.com/rss/news_285.rss (Economy/Macro focus)
* MarketWatch (Bulletin): Known for extremely fast, short-burst headlines on market "shocks."
    * URL: http://feeds.marketwatch.com/marketwatch/bulletins
* Financial Times (Global Economy): Higher quality, lower noise. Best for long-term macro sentiment.
    * URL: https://www.ft.com/global-economy?format=rss

2. Asset-Specific Breaking News
To get news on specific stocks (e.g., NVDA, AAPL, TSLA), you need feeds that pull directly from regulatory filings or press releases.
* Yahoo Finance (Ticker Specific): You can actually generate a feed for any specific ticker.
    * Formula: https://finance.yahoo.com/rss/headline?s=TICKER
    * Example for NVIDIA: https://finance.yahoo.com/rss/headline?s=NVDA
* SEC Filings (EDGAR): This is the ultimate "Asset-Specific" source. It is the first place a company must report "Material Events" (8-K filings).
    * URL: https://www.sec.gov/cgi-bin/browse-edgar?action=getcurrent&type=8-K&owner=include&output=atom
* PR Newswire (Finance): This is where companies blast their earnings reports and merger news before it hits the mainstream media.
    * URL: https://www.prnewswire.com/rss/financial-services-latest-news/financial-services-latest-news-list.rss

3. Geopolitical & "Risk-On/Off" (New for 2026)
Given the current 2026 climate (e.g., the Iran-US trade tensions and AI "disillusionment" mentioned in recent reports), these feeds act as early warning systems.
* Reuters (World News): Essential for "Risk-Off" sentiment (e.g., war news affecting oil).
    * URL: https://www.reutersagency.com/feed/?best-topics=world
* S&P Global (Commodity/Energy News): Specifically for tracking the "Energy Shock" sentiment of 2026.
    * URL: https://www.spglobal.com/energy/en/news-research/rss-feed
